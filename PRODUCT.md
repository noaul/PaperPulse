# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PaperPulse is for researchers, engineers, and technical readers who monitor journal RSS feeds, run AI-assisted relevance analysis, review paper summaries, and turn high-signal papers into reports or reading queues.

Users arrive with a task already in mind: check what changed today, understand whether analysis jobs finished, inspect paper relevance, and decide what to read or send next.

## Product Purpose

PaperPulse exists to make literature monitoring observable and repeatable. It connects RSS ingestion, keyword matching, AI analysis, report generation, email delivery, WebDAV backup, workspaces, and a Zotero prototype into one local-first workflow.

Success means the user can quickly answer: what was fetched, what was analyzed, what matters, what failed, and what needs attention.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, research-grade.

The interface should feel like a focused research operations console: trustworthy, readable, and efficient. It should avoid drama, oversized marketing composition, and decorative styling that competes with dense scientific content.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI-dashboard styling, purple glow gradients, over-dark xAI cosplay, decorative glass cards, oversized rounded panels, weak gray text, and UI that forces light Tailwind components through broad global overrides.

Avoid marketing-page composition. PaperPulse is a working app, not a landing page.

## Design Principles

1. Prioritize scan speed: titles, counts, statuses, and actions should be easy to compare at a glance.
2. Preserve task muscle memory: keep routes, navigation labels, and core workflows stable unless a change is explicitly requested.
3. Make workflow state legible: running, paused, failed, sent, analyzed, and empty states should be clear without reading long prose.
4. Keep visual energy restrained: use color for primary action, selection, and status, not decoration.
5. Support long reading sessions: text contrast, spacing, and density should reduce fatigue when reviewing many papers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve keyboard focus visibility, avoid motion that blocks task flow, and make status colors readable with text labels so color is not the only signal.
